02 — Talking to the System

Use It However You Work

You don't stop what you're doing to use software. You're elbow-deep in prep, you're expediting, you're talking to a vendor on the phone. The system meets you wherever you are.

Walk-in

Phone or Tablet

Check inventory from the walk-in cooler. Approve a vendor order between lunch and dinner service. Pull up a recipe while you're plating. Works offline — syncs when you're back in range.

On the line

Voice Commands

"We're 86'd on the halibut. Text the fish guy for tomorrow delivery and pull it from tonight's specials." Your hands are covered in flour. The system handles it.

After close

Computer

End-of-night reconciliation. Review today's food cost. Plan next week's specials. Adjust par levels based on what moved and what didn't. Everything in one place.

Vendor call

Messaging

A vendor texts you a deal on short ribs. Forward it to the system — it checks your menu, calculates the margin, and tells you if it's worth it. Text back "order 40 lbs" and it's done.

Automatic

Scheduled Tasks

Daily prep lists generated at 5am. Auto-orders triggered when stock hits par levels. Weekly food cost reports every Monday morning. Temp log reminders every two hours during service.

04 — A Day in the Life

Real Situations, Handled

These aren't hypotheticals. Every independent restaurant deals with exactly these situations every single week. Here's how the system handles each one.

⚠ Mid-service — the rush is on

86'd and Covered

You run out of salmon mid-service. System texts your fish vendor for next-day delivery, suggests an alternative for tonight's specials board, and sends a heads-up to front of house so they stop selling it before another ticket comes in.

AI + Front of House
☎ 4:00 PM — Dinner prep, phone rings

Call-Out Scramble

Your line cook calls out at 4pm for tonight's dinner shift. System checks your roster for who's off today, texts the three most likely to pick up a shift, and starts building a simplified prep plan in case nobody can cover.

AI + Your Staff
↻ Tuesday morning — invoice day

Vendor Price Hike

Your produce vendor raises prices on three items. System flags it immediately, pulls pricing from your other vendors for the same products, and calculates the impact on your menu food cost. You see it all before the next order goes out.

AI — automatic
↻ Every Monday — compliance check

Health Inspection Prep

Weekly automated sweep: are temperature logs current? Cleaning schedules signed off? ServSafe certs up to date? Walk-in organized per FIFO? Generates a single report of what needs attention before anyone from the health department shows up.

AI — automatic
☾ 11:30 PM — Closing time

End of Night

Closing manager does the inventory count. System calculates today's actual food cost against projected, flags anything approaching spoilage or expiration, and auto-generates tomorrow's prep list based on reservations and projected walk-ins.

AI + Closing Manager
☁ Thursday — planning ahead

Weekend Projections

Based on current reservations, weather forecast, and your historical cover counts: projected covers for Friday through Sunday, suggested prep quantities for each station, and recommended staffing levels. You adjust what you want and approve the rest.

AI — automatic

How the whole cycle works — new seasonal menu item
You say:     "I want to run a braised lamb shank for fall. Serve it
              over polenta with gremolata. Let's see if the numbers work."

The system:  Creates a menu item record for Braised Lamb Shank
              Pulls current lamb pricing from your vendors
              Costs the full plate: protein, polenta, gremolata, garnish
              Calculates target menu price at your food cost percentage
              Sends you a summary: "Lamb shank — $6.40 plate cost, $24 menu price = 26.7%"

You approve: "Run it. Order lamb for Friday. 30 portions."

The system:  Places the order with your meat vendor for Friday AM delivery
              Generates a prep sheet: braising instructions, polenta batch size, gremolata mise
              Adds it to the specials board template for front of house
              Tracks nightly sales and actual vs. projected food cost
              Reports back Sunday: "Lamb shank — 24 sold, 6 wasted, actual cost 28.1%"
